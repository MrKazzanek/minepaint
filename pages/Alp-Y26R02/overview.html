Overview

AssetsPacks are special resource packs for the program that allow modification of the visual and structural content of the game world without changing the program’s functionality. They work similarly to mods or resource packs in Minecraft, but with clear limitations – they cannot add new functions, tools, or modify game mechanics. AssetsPacks are only for changing the appearance and layout of elements in the game.

What can you do with AssetsPacks?

  • Change textures of existing blocks, objects, and weather overlays.
  • Add new blocks, blockstates, and categories, expanding the program’s content.
  • Edit existing blocks, blockstates, and categories, e.g., changing their properties or appearance.
  • Rearrange blocks and categories in the inventory, both custom and existing, to suit your preferences.
  • Remove blocks and categories that are unnecessary or should be replaced.

What cannot be done?

  • You cannot change the program’s functionality or add new features.
  • You cannot create custom tools or mechanics – that’s why AssetsPacks are not full-fledged mods.

Installing AssetsPacks

AssetsPacks can be installed in two ways:

  1. Via the AssetsPack Manager:
    • Click the AP button to open the pack manager.
    • You will see a list of available packs and packs that are already enabled.
    • Below the list, there are two buttons:
      • Refresh List – refreshes the list and loads the pack contents.
      • Open Folder – opens the folder where the pack should be copied.
    • After adding a pack, use Refresh List to activate it.
    • Multiple packs can be enabled at the same time.
  2. Directly in the program folder:

    Copy the pack folder to the location:

    C:\Users\User\AppData\Roaming\Godot\app_userdata\MinePaint\assets_packs

    Then refresh the list in the manager so the pack appears in the list.

Activating Packs and Priority

  • To enable a pack, check its checkbox in the AssetsPack manager.
  • An enabled pack will be highlighted in green.
  • Once enabled, Up/Down buttons appear to set the pack’s order.
  • Pack Priority: Packs higher in the order have priority in conflicts – e.g., if two packs modify the texture of the same block, the one higher in priority will be applied.

Summary

AssetsPacks are a powerful tool for customizing the program – they allow enhancing visual content, adding custom blocks and categories, and organizing the inventory without risking program functionality. The priority system allows you to control which packs take precedence, giving full flexibility in creating and managing your own resource packs.
